## Context

choicer v0.1.0 shipped a Bayesian MNP (`run_mnprobit`) with a heavily optimized C++ Gibbs engine: single persistent OpenMP region, per-(iteration, observation) xoshiro256++ RNG streams (bitwise thread-count invariance), three-regime truncated-normal sampler, conjugate draws via Cholesky solves, Gram-block precomputation, abort/interrupt protocol, and per-draw McCulloch–Rossi identification on the R side. This plan adds the two hierarchical workhorses of applied discrete choice on the same engine pattern:

1. **HMNL** — hierarchical (random-coefficients, panel) multinomial logit, `run_hmnlogit()`, class `choicer_hmnl`
2. **HMNP** — hierarchical multinomial probit with **iid N(0, σ²)** utility shocks (no error covariance matrix, single variance parameter), `run_hmnprobit()`, class `choicer_hmnp`

**Target applications** (these drive several design choices below): hospital choice (WTP, counterfactual choice/entry), school & college choice (subsidy-policy counterfactuals), and general demand estimation. The common thread is welfare and counterfactuals over **many alternatives and many choice situations**, which is the regime this design is tuned for.

### Design decisions (this revision)

The original draft treated ASCs as individual-level coordinates inside `β_i ~ N(b, W)` (dense ASC dummy columns, a `(K_struct + J − 1) × (·)` covariance). That does not scale: it carries J−1 free population-mean ASCs, an O(J²) ASC covariance block, and an O((K_struct+J)³) per-respondent Cholesky. This revision **decouples the alternative dimension from the respondent dimension** — the change that makes large-J tractable:

- **β_i ~ N(b, W) carries STRUCTURAL TASTES ONLY.** W is `K_struct × K_struct` (small, the genuine mixed-logit object). No ASCs in β_i, none in W.
- **δ_j is a global, alternative-level random effect with a BLP mean function:** δ_j = z_j′θ + ξ_j, ξ_j ~ N(0, σ_d²). One scalar variance σ_d² regardless of J; ξ_j is the micro-Bayesian counterpart of BLP's unobserved product quality. Partial pooling shrinks each δ_j toward its characteristics-based mean z_j′θ — consistent under sparse per-alternative data where fixed dummies are noisy/inconsistent (incidental-parameters in the alternative dimension), and it yields a **posterior-predictive δ for alternatives not in the estimation sample** (entry counterfactuals).
- **First-class outside option** (systematic utility fixed at 0; each model's own utility shock applies). This is the location anchor for δ (see Identification) and is required for welfare/CV in all three applications. δ_j is then *mean utility relative to the outside good*. The outside option reuses the package's existing convention: `include_outside_option = TRUE` + `outside_opt_label`, with the outside modelled **implicitly** (no physical rows; see Data prep).
- **Global δ_j (not market-specific δ_jm) in v1.** A market/segment index is roadmapped; the prep is written so it can be retrofitted.
- **Price/policy-variable endogeneity via a control-function hook:** the wrapper accepts a user-supplied first-stage residual column (Petrin–Train 2010) that enters utility as an ordinary covariate. No sampler changes; documented assumption. Joint Bayesian-IV (price equation sampled alongside) is roadmapped.
- **Full posterior-draws post-estimation suite on the HB classes**, registered as methods on the package's existing generics: `predict(newdata=)` (incl. new alternatives), `wtp()`, `consumer_surplus()` + `logsum()` (HMNL-only in v1), `elasticities()` + diversion — each returning posterior distributions, not delta-method points. These ARE the deliverables for the target applications.

**Sampler decisions carried over:** HMNL uses adaptive RW-Metropolis-within-Gibbs (no Pólya-Gamma). HMNP samples σ² (conjugate IG) in a non-identified chain and normalizes every kept draw by σ — the MR1994 pattern doubling as PX-DA for better mixing (this is the **scale** normalization; see Identification). Distributions: HMNL supports normal + log-normal **structural** coordinates (coefficient enters utility as exp(β_ik); hierarchy normal on log scale); HMNP normal-only (preserves full conjugacy). δ_j and its hierarchy are always normal.

**Data structure — cross-section OR panel, one code path:** both models are written for respondents i with T_i ≥ 1 choice situations sharing one β_i. **Panel data is NOT required and not assumed**: the `t` index is generality, not a constraint — `person_col = NULL` (the default) makes each choice situation its own respondent (T_i = 1), the cross-sectional random-coefficients model, i.e. the Bayesian counterpart of the package's own `run_mxlogit`, targeting the same (b, W). The samplers are identical in both modes (the within-person task loop simply has one iteration). The δ_j level is **global and shared across all respondents and situations regardless of mode**, so even at T_i = 1 the alternative-level random effect is informed by every choice of every respondent — a key reason this structure is robust in cross-sections. Statistical expectations by mode: b and (θ, σ_d², δ) ≈ equally well estimated either way; W identified in cross-sections via covariate/choice-set variation and functional form (Train Ch. 6) but slower-concentrating, more IW-prior-sensitive (fragile only in the binary-choice T=1 corner); individual β_i ≈ shrinkage to b at T=1 (a data limitation any method shares). The package currently has NO grouping notion (`id_col` = one situation everywhere), so the two-level indexing below is new infrastructure either way.

## Models

Let `x_ijt` (K_struct-vector) be structural attributes, `z_j` (P-vector) be alternative-level covariates (the mean-function design), and let the **outside option** o carry systematic utility 0 (no x, no δ) plus the model's utility shock. Choice sets may be unbalanced and may differ across tasks.

**HMNL**: U_ijt = x_ijt′γ_i + δ_j + EV1 for inside alternatives; U_iot = EV1.
- γ_ik = β_ik or exp(β_ik) per `rc_dist` (structural coords only).
- **Respondent level:** β_i ~ N(b, W), W is `K_struct × K_struct`. Priors b ~ N(b̄, A⁻¹) (defaults 0, 0.01·I), W ~ IW(ν, V) (defaults ν = K_struct+3, V = ν·I — matches `R/mnprobit_utils.R:149-152`).
- **Alternative level:** δ_j = z_j′θ + ξ_j, ξ_j ~ N(0, σ_d²). Priors θ ~ N(θ̄, A_θ⁻¹) (defaults 0, 0.01·I); **σ_d ~ half-Cauchy(0, s_d)** implemented as a conjugate IG scale-mixture (auxiliary a_d: σ_d² | a_d ~ IG(½, 1/a_d), a_d ~ IG(½, 1/s_d²); Makalic–Schmidt 2016, in the half-t family recommended by Gelman 2006) to avoid the IG(ε,ε) near-zero pathology; an IG(c0,d0) fallback is exposed.
- **Identification:** EV1 fixes scale; the outside option fixes location of δ (see below). Reported directly.
- **Gibbs** (per iteration):
  (a) per-respondent RW-MH on β_i, proposal cov s_i²·(H_i + W⁻¹)⁻¹, H_i = respondent information at the pooled MNL MLE (computed once at startup over **structural coords only**; Jacobian-adjusted to log scale for log-normal coords: H_i = J·H_i^γ·J, J = diag(exp(β_pooled,k))); s_i Robbins–Monro-adapted toward 0.234 during burn-in, **frozen at r == burn**; cached current loglik ⇒ ONE likelihood eval/step;
  (b) b | β,W conjugate MVN (precision A + N·W⁻¹);
  (c) W | β,b ~ IW(ν+N, V + Σ(β_i−b)(β_i−b)′);
  (d) **δ_j | rest** — logit, non-conjugate ⇒ adaptive RW-MH, **SERIAL sweep over j** (the δ_j conditionals are coupled through the shared per-task softmax denominators, so a work-shared/synchronous update across j would not leave the posterior invariant; each δ_j update must see the latest δ). The conditional is log-concave (logit likelihood × Gaussian prior N(z_j′θ, σ_d²)). Cost stays O(#tasks containing j) per alternative via a per-task denominator cache held in level form: Δloglik needs only the affected tasks' cached D_t and the old/new exp(v_jt) terms, and an accepted move updates each affected D_t in O(1). The caches are rebuilt in a fixed-order work-shared pass at the start of every iteration (required anyway because the β-phase changed the linear predictors), which bounds incremental FP drift to a single sweep;
  (e) θ | δ, σ_d² conjugate MVN (Bayesian regression of δ on Z, error var σ_d²);
  (f) σ_d² | δ, θ conjugate IG via the scale-mixture (or IG fallback).
- Init: pooled MNL MLE (structural coords) via existing frequentist kernels; δ_j init from log choice-share contrasts vs the outside option with an add-half shrinkage clamp ((n_j + 0.5)-style) so never-chosen alternatives do not produce −Inf; θ init from an OLS of δ_init on Z.

**HMNP (iid)**: U_ijt = x_ijt′β_i + δ_j + ε for inside alternatives, **U_iot = ε** (stochastic — NOT a deterministic 0; see Identification and the review note), ε ~ iid N(0, σ²); choice = argmax in task. **Un-differenced utility space** ⇒ iid structure kept, univariate truncated-normal augmentation, **unbalanced choice sets supported**. All conjugate:
- (a) U_ijt | rest ~ univariate TN (chosen alt: lower = current max of others **including the outside latent at its current value**; others incl. the outside latent: upper = current U_chosen) via existing `rtruncnorm`. The outside latent is one more TN draw per task in the same sweep (mean 0, no x, no δ) — it is a full participant in the augmentation, not a fixed bound;
- (b) β_i | U_i, b, W, σ², δ conjugate regression on the δ-residualized utilities, precision W⁻¹ + X_i′X_i/σ² with **X_i′X_i precomputed once**;
- (c)(d) b, W as in HMNL (shared code);
- (e) **δ_j | rest** conjugate Normal — given augmented U and β_i, δ_j is Gaussian-linear: precision n_j/σ² + 1/σ_d² (n_j = #rows where j appears), mean from the residuals r_ijt = U_ijt − x_ijt′β_i pooled over those rows plus the prior pull z_j′θ/σ_d²; δ_j **conditionally independent across j given U** ⇒ validly drawn in one work-shared parallel pass (this independence is what HMNL's softmax coupling lacks — the asymmetry is documented in `hb_internal.h` and the math notes);
- (f) θ | δ, σ_d² conjugate MVN; (g) σ_d² | δ, θ conjugate IG (scale-mixture);
- (h) σ² | U, β, δ ~ IG(a₀ + n_rows/2, s₀ + RSS/2), defaults a₀ = 3, s₀ = 3, where **outside latents count in n_rows and contribute U_iot² to RSS** (their residual is the latent itself).

**Identification — two distinct redundancies, handled differently:**
- **Scale (multiplicative; HMNP σ only).** The probit likelihood is invariant to common rescaling of (U, σ). Run the **non-identified σ chain** and divide every kept draw by σ (the σ_11/MR1994 pattern; genuine PX-DA ⇒ improves mixing). Report b/σ, W/σ², δ_j/σ, θ/σ, σ_d²/σ², β_i/σ; raw chains stored (mirrors `draws$beta_raw`). HMNL has no scale parameter (EV1 fixes it) ⇒ no scale normalization.
- **Location (additive; both models).** Adding a constant to all inside δ_j cancels in the softmax — UNLESS an outside option with systematic utility 0 breaks it. Because v1 includes a **first-class outside option**, the δ level is **genuinely identified** (δ_j = mean utility vs the outside good) and the mean-function intercept θ_0 is identified (average inside-good quality vs outside). **No post-hoc location normalization, no sum-to-zero constraint, no arbitrary base alternative.** (If a future no-outside-good mode is added, θ_0 is dropped and ξ is centered — documented in the math notes, not built in v1.) ξ_j ~ N(0, σ_d²) are then genuine mean-zero deviations from the characteristics-based mean — the BLP reading.
- **Why the HMNP outside shock is stochastic (ε, not deterministic 0):** (i) parallelism with HMNL, whose outside good necessarily carries an EV1 shock — this is what makes the HMNP-vs-HMNL π/√6 comparability check meaningful; (ii) with iid shocks on all J+1 alternatives, utilities differenced against the outside are equicorrelated (var 2σ², cov σ²), the exact probit analog of logit-with-outside — a deterministic outside gives independent differenced errors (var σ²), a subtly different model; (iii) the √2-vs-binary-probit test below already embraces the both-shocks convention; the cost is one extra TN draw per task.

## Data prep (cross-section or panel; grouping optional; outside option first-class)

`prepare_h*_data(data, id_col, alt_col, choice_col, covariate_cols, person_col = NULL, alt_covariate_cols = NULL, outside_opt_label = NULL, cf_residual_col = NULL, include_outside_option = TRUE[, rc_dist])` sorts by (person, id, alt_int) and returns classed `choicer_data_hmnl/hmnp`. Argument names deliberately match the existing frequentist prep (`prepare_mnl_data`) so the outside-option convention is package-wide:
- `X` (total_rows × K_struct): structural covariates **only** — NO ASC dummy columns (the decoupling). If `cf_residual_col` is supplied it is appended to X as an ordinary covariate (control function; its coefficient absorbs price endogeneity). **δ_j is NOT in X** — it is indexed separately.
- `alt_of_row` (integer alternative code per row, 1..J for inside rows) — δ_j is a length-J vector gathered by this index, not a dense design block. This is the memory fix at large J. **The outside option is implicit**: as in `prepare_mnl_data` (`R/mnlogit_utils.R:432`), physical outside rows (identified by `outside_opt_label`) are removed and the kernels add the outside term (HMNL: exp(0) in each task's denominator; HMNP: one implicit outside latent per task, a length-n_tasks vector). No 0 code in `alt_of_row`; the outside choice is encoded in `choice_pos` following the existing 0 = outside/base convention.
- `Z` (J × P): alternative-level mean-function design from `alt_covariate_cols`, one row per inside alternative (deduplicated, constant-within-alternative checked). Defaults to an intercept-only Z (P = 1) when `alt_covariate_cols = NULL`, so θ_0 is the common inside-good level vs outside.
- `M` (n_tasks, counting **inside** rows; with the implicit outside the effective choice set is M+1 — require M ≥ 1 when `include_outside_option`, M ≥ 2 otherwise), `choice_pos` (within task; 0 = outside chosen).
- `alt_idx` (for the pooled-MLE init), `Ti`, `person_ids`, `N_persons`, `n_tasks`, `J` (inside alts), `K_struct`, `P`, `alt_mapping`, `param_map` (list(beta=…, theta=…), robust to collinearity drops), `rc_dist` (structural-K vector), `dropped_cols`, `data_spec` (incl. person_col, outside_opt_label, cf_residual_col, alt_covariate_cols).
- Validations: exactly one choice per task; NA-task dropping with warnings; **covariates constant within every task are dropped ONLY when `include_outside_option = FALSE`** (without an outside good they are unidentified and flatten the pooled MLE / make H_i singular; WITH a first-class outside good they shift all inside utilities relative to the outside and are genuinely identified — kept, with an informational message; the pooled-MLE init passes `include_outside_option = TRUE` through the existing kernel so the MLE is well-posed); covariate collinearity via the invisible QR check; Z columns constant across alternatives dropped (except the intercept); cf_residual presence noted in provenance; warn if price-like covariate present without a cf_residual (endogeneity reminder).

## C++ kernel design (both follow the mnprobit template, `src/mnprobit.cpp:260-434`)

- **One persistent `#pragma omp parallel` region** for the whole chain; work-shared `schedule(static)` loops over respondents (β_i, augmentation) and — **HMNP only** — over alternatives (δ_j conjugate draws, valid by conditional independence given U); the **HMNL δ sweep runs serially on the master thread** between barriers (coupled conditionals; see Models (d)); `#pragma omp master` + barriers for hierarchy draws (b, W, θ, σ_d², σ²), recording, trace, interrupt poll (every 100 iters); abort-enum protocol with `Rcpp::stop` after the region; worker-side failures (proposal Cholesky) set the abort code in a `#pragma omp critical` cold path.
- **RNG partition** per iteration r (documented in headers + math docs): tag i = respondent i's latent/MH work; tag N+i = β_i regression draw (HMNP); tag 2N + j = δ_j draw (j = 1..J); tags **2N + J + {0, 1, 2, 3}** = b, W, θ, (σ_d², σ²) on master — based past the δ block so the partition is collision-free for any J, including the J > N regime the design targets. `make_stream(seed, r, tag)` (`src/rng.h:129`).
- **Bitwise thread invariance:** every per-respondent and per-alternative quantity computed from own slots + master-published state in fixed internal order; ALL cross-respondent accumulations (Σβ_i, scatter S, RSS, loglik trace) AND **cross-respondent per-δ_j sufficient statistics** (Σ residuals and counts over rows with alt j) reduced on master in fixed i = 0..N−1 / row order. The HMNL serial δ sweep is trivially thread-count invariant (master-only); its cached denominators are rebuilt in a fixed-order work-shared pass each iteration. Same contract as `src/mnprobit.cpp:20-35`, tested 1-vs-4 threads (now including δ_j, θ, σ_d²).
- **HMNL loop body:** (cache rebuild, work-shared) recompute per-task linear predictors, level-form denominators D_t (incl. the outside exp(0) term), and per-respondent cached logliks in fixed order — needed every iteration because the β-phase changes them. (β-phase, work-shared over respondents) P = H_i + W⁻¹ → hand-rolled chol → candidate β* = β_i + s_i·chol⁻ᵀz → one fixed-order loglik pass using the cached δ-augmented linear predictor (LSE per task incl. outside 0; non-finite ⇒ −inf auto-reject) → MH accept with prior quad-forms → adapt log s_i, clamp [log 0.01, log 10], freeze post-burn. (δ-phase, after barrier, SERIAL on master) for j = 1..J: RW-MH on δ_j against tasks containing j — Δloglik from cached D_t plus old/new exp(v_jt), O(1) per affected task; on accept, update the affected D_t (and task logliks) in place so δ_{j+1} sees the latest state; Gaussian prior N(z_j′θ, σ_d²), own adapted step. (master) draw b, W, θ, σ_d²; record.
- **HMNP loop body (fused):** per respondent in ONE work-shared pass — TN sweep over the respondent's rows **plus the task's implicit outside latent** (fixed sub-order; the outside latent is drawn like any other row — mean 0, no x, no δ — and its current value enters the other rows' truncation bounds) → β_i conjugate draw on δ-residualized utilities (precision W⁻¹ + G_i/σ², two trisolves) → accumulate per-j residual sufficient stats into master-reduced buffers → rss_i (**outside latents included**: each contributes U_iot² to RSS and 1 to the row count). Barrier; work-shared δ_j conjugate draws (precision n_j/σ² + 1/σ_d²); barrier; master draws b, W, θ, σ_d², σ² (IG via 1/rgamma; scale-mixture aux for σ_d²) and records.
- **Precomputation:** HMNP Gram cube G_i = X_i′X_i (structural K only ⇒ much smaller than the original dense-ASC version); per-alternative row-index lists (CSR over alt_of_row); HMNL H_i cube (structural K) + per-alternative task lists at startup (work-shared, deterministic).
- **Storage:** thinned draws only; `keep_beta_i ∈ {none=0, means=1, draws=2}` (Welford online for "means", identified scale for HMNP); δ_j and ξ_j = δ_j − z_j′θ always summarized (means/sd) and optionally returned as draws (the quality ladder); "draws" β_i returns a (K_struct, N, R_keep) cube.
- Hotspots/iter ≈ N·K_struct³/3 (hand-rolled chols, now K_struct not K_struct+J) + one serial δ sweep costing O(total task-alt incidences) with O(1) per incidence (HMNL) or one work-shared conjugate δ pass (HMNP) + one likelihood/augmentation pass over total_rows. The δ-decoupling removes the dominant O((K_struct+J)³) term — the core scalability win.

## R wrappers

`run_hmnlogit` / `run_hmnprobit(data, id_col, alt_col, choice_col, covariate_cols, person_col = NULL, alt_covariate_cols = NULL, outside_opt_label = NULL, cf_residual_col = NULL, input_data = NULL, base_alt = NULL, include_outside_option = TRUE, [rc_dist,] prior = list(), mcmc = list(), chains = 1, keep_beta_i = c("means","draws","none"), keep_data = TRUE)`:
1. Dual pathway resolution (clone of `R/mnprobit_utils.R:115-145`); prior/mcmc merged over defaults with dimension checks (mcmc defaults R=10000, burn=R%/%5, thin=1, seed=NULL→`sample.int` so `set.seed()` governs, trace=0; HMNL extra: s_init=2.38/sqrt(K_struct), accept_target=0.234; sd_prior default half-Cauchy scale s_d). `chains > 1` ⇒ run independent chains (seeds offset; optionally parallel via the existing future plan) and compute split-R̂.
2. HMNL only — **pooled MNL MLE init** (structural coords) using existing kernels: `mnl_loglik_gradient_parallel(..., include_outside_option = TRUE)` through `run_optimizer()` (`R/classes.R:318`); log-normal coords transformed to chain scale with warn-and-clamp `log(pmax(γ̂_k, 0.05))`. δ_init from clamped log share-vs-outside contrasts; θ_init from OLS of δ_init on Z.
3. Call kernel(s) inside `system.time`; `message("MCMC run time ", convertTime(...))`.
4. HMNP post-processing — **per-draw scale normalization** (discipline of `R/mnprobit_utils.R:189-209`): divide b, W, δ, θ, σ_d², β_i draws by the matching power of σ; summaries from identified draws; raw chains kept in `draws$*_raw`. (No location step — outside option identifies it.)
5. Build posterior object: coefficients/se/vcov = mean/sd/cov of identified b draws; θ summaries (mean-function); σ_d² summary; posterior-mean W (labeled); `delta` and `xi` summaries (+ optional draws) labeled by alternative; `beta_i` (means/sd + person_ids, or cube); `accept` (β and δ, HMNL); R̂ table; nobs = n_tasks, plus n_persons, J, P; sampler metadata; `cf` provenance (whether a control-function residual was included). Warnings: HMNL mean acceptance ∉ (0.10, 0.60); N_persons < K_struct (W prior-driven); J small relative to Z columns (θ/σ_d² prior-driven); any R̂ > 1.05; memory guard for keep_beta_i="draws" (warn >1 GB, stop >4 GB).

## Post-estimation (the deliverables)

All registered as methods on the **existing** package generics, integrating over posterior draws (and over the relevant population/individual level), returning **posterior distributions** (point = posterior quantiles/mean as noted):
- **`predict(object, newdata, level = c("population","individual"), integrate = TRUE)`** — counterfactual choice probabilities/shares. `population` integrates β over N(b, W) (Monte Carlo over draws and over a β draw per posterior draw); `individual` uses per-respondent β_i. **New alternatives** in `newdata` (not in estimation) get δ from the **posterior-predictive** N(z_new′θ, σ_d²) — the entry-counterfactual capability the random-effects δ unlocks. Subsidy/price counterfactuals are just modified covariate columns. **HMNP probabilities cannot reuse the logit kernels**: with iid errors they are the 1-D integral P(j) = ∫ φ(u) Π_{k≠j} Φ((V_j − V_k)/σ + u) du (outside: V = 0), evaluated by fixed-node Gauss–Hermite quadrature — one shared helper serves predict, elasticities, and diversion for `choicer_hmnp`.
- **`wtp(object, price_var, attr_vars)`** — posterior distribution of −β_attr/β_price (handles log-normal/sign-constrained price; see roadmap). With a normal price coefficient the ratio posterior is heavy-tailed and its mean may not exist: report the **posterior median + equal-tailed quantile CI** (never the mean) and **warn when P(β_price ≥ 0) exceeds a small threshold** (sign-indeterminate price ⇒ WTP ill-defined). No delta method.
- **`consumer_surplus(object, price_var, newdata)` + `logsum(object, newdata)`** — compensating variation via logsum differences (Train Ch. 3), full posterior; the workhorse for subsidy-policy welfare. **HMNL-only in v1** (`consumer_surplus.choicer_hmnl`, `logsum.choicer_hmnl`): the logsum formula is EV1-specific and probit E[max U] has no closed form — the simulated-Emax HMNP variant is roadmapped, and the `choicer_hmnp` method signposts it with an informative error.
- **`elasticities(object)` + `diversion_ratios(object)`** — own/cross elasticities and diversion, posterior summaries; for demand estimation and merger-style counterfactuals (HMNP via the quadrature helper).
- **Quality ladder:** `summary` and a `coef(object, "delta"/"xi")` accessor expose the δ_j (mean utility vs outside) and ξ_j (unobserved quality) posteriors.

## DGPs & recovery

`simulate_hmnl_data(N=500, T=10, J=4, beta, W=NULL, theta, sigma_d, Z=NULL, rc_dist=NULL, include_outside=TRUE, seed, vary_choice_set=FALSE)` and `simulate_hmnp_data(..., sigma=1)` → `choicer_sim`; truth stored for b, W, θ, σ_d, and the realized δ_j (and ξ_j); HMNP truth on the identified scale (b/σ, W/σ², δ/σ, θ/σ, σ_d²/σ²); HMNL log-normal structural truth on the chain (log) scale. DGP draws δ_j = z_j′θ + ξ_j and optionally an outside option (with its own utility shock, matching the model); supports `vary_choice_set` and a held-out alternative for the entry-prediction test. `recovery_table.choicer_hb` compares the beta/w/theta/sigma_d blocks plus a δ-recovery block.

## Edge cases & risks

- **Outside option is the location anchor:** with it, δ-level and θ_0 are identified; document the no-outside-good corner (would require sum-to-zero on ξ / dropping θ_0) as out-of-scope for v1.
- **σ_d² / θ identification:** driven by cross-alternative variation in choice frequencies beyond Z. With few alternatives (J small) or thin per-alt data they lean on the prior — message; half-Cauchy(σ_d) avoids the IG near-zero pathology.
- **N_persons < K_struct:** IW posterior proper but prior-driven — message, document.
- **Cross-sectional usage (T_i = 1, person_col = NULL):** fully supported; (b, W, θ, σ_d²) identified as in frequentist MXL + BLP; individual β_i posteriors ≈ N(b, W) tilted by one choice (document for keep_beta_i users); δ_j robust because global. Compute negligible.
- **Control function:** the cf residual is a covariate only; **the wrapper does not run the first stage** — document that the user must supply a valid first-stage residual and that, absent it, δ_j (= z_j′θ + ξ_j) is exogenous-only-conditional-on-Z (loud roxygen warning on `wtp`/`consumer_surplus`). Standard errors do not currently propagate first-stage uncertainty (roadmap: joint Bayesian IV).
- **Log-normal overflow (HMNL):** non-finite candidate loglik ⇒ −inf (auto-reject); startup loglik checked finite or abort.
- **Adaptation pathology:** clamped log s_i / s_δ, NaN acceptance ⇒ 0, frozen post-burn; s_final + per-respondent and per-alternative acceptance returned.
- **σ² drift (HMNP non-identified chain):** IG(3,3) + A=0.01·I keep it proper; sigma2 trace stored.
- **δ-hierarchy funnel (small σ_d²):** the centered parameterization (sample δ, then θ | δ conjugate) can mix slowly when σ_d² is small; acceptable for v1 given it preserves θ/σ_d² conjugacy — ASIS/interweaving is roadmapped if δ mixing proves poor at small J.
- **Ordering consistency:** single source of truth = prep's (person, id, alt) sort; chosen_row, alt_of_row, and CSR alt lists derived from the same offsets; pooled-MLE init reuses identical X/M/choice_pos.

## Roadmap (designed-around, not built in v1)

- **Joint Bayesian IV** (price equation sampled alongside; Jiang–Manchanda–Rossi 2009) replacing the control-function hook for full uncertainty propagation.
- **Market-specific δ_jm** via a market/segment index in the prep (the `alt_of_row`/Z indexing is written to extend to (alt, market)).
- **Sampling of alternatives** (McFadden) for very large J — consistent for logit (extra reason HMNL is the large-J workhorse over probit); the δ-residualized likelihood pass is the integration point.
- **Sign-constrained / WTP-space price coefficient** (log-normal price so WTP is well-defined per draw; Train–Weeks 2005) for robust WTP/CV.
- **Simulated-Emax `logsum`/`consumer_surplus` for HMNP** (probit E[max] has no closed form; Monte Carlo or 1-D quadrature over the iid shocks).
- **ASIS/interweaving for the δ hierarchy** if centered-parameterization mixing is poor at small σ_d².
- **Non-normal ξ_j** (t / DP-mixture) for fat-tailed quality.
